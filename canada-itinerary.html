Step one

You're not doing all four cities — pick the second half

Banff anchors the first 5 nights either way. The only real decision is what comes after Calgary: a direct hop to Toronto, or a flight to Montréal with a side trip to Québec City. Quick note up front — Banff sits in the Rocky Mountains in Alberta (Western Canada), while Toronto/Québec/Montréal are in central & eastern Canada, so this trip really has two distinct halves no matter which you pick.

Route A — Banff + Toronto

Flight from Calgary
~3h45m nonstop to Toronto (YYZ)
Vibe
Big-city skyline, CN Tower, Niagara Falls — one international flight home, no internal transfers
Best for
First-timers, families who want "two icons in one trip" (mountains + Niagara), and the simplest logistics
Trade-off
You'll miss the Old World cobblestones and French-Canadian culture of Québec

Route B — Banff + Québec City & Montréal

Flight from Calgary
~4h15–4h30m nonstop to Montréal (YUL), plus a ~3h train or drive to Québec City and back
Vibe
Walled Old Town, French language & food culture, slower-paced and very walkable
Best for
Families who want history, architecture, and food over big-city sightseeing — July is also festival season in Québec
Trade-off
One extra travel day (the Québec ⇄ Montréal leg), and a bit more packing/unpacking

Everything below — itinerary, hotels, attractions, map — follows whichever route is selected.

The plan

Day by day

Days 1–5 are the same either way: Banff, Lake Louise, and the Icefields Parkway. Day 6 onward depends on your route. The line below is a (very rough) elevation profile of the trip — the Rockies really are that much higher than everywhere else you're going.

01Calgary → Banff

Arrive & settle in

Land in Calgary (YYC), drive the Trans-Canada to Banff (~1h25 / 130km), check into your hotel. Easy first evening: wander Banff Avenue, sunset at Bow Falls & Surprise Corner.

02Lake Louise

Lake Louise & Moraine Lake

The big one — book your Parks Canada shuttle reservation as early as possible (see Good to Know). Canoe on the lake if you have time and energy left.

03Banff townsite

Gondola, canyon & hot springs

Banff Gondola up Sulphur Mountain in the morning, Johnston Canyon catwalk hike to the falls midday, Banff Upper Hot Springs or Cave and Basin to finish.

04Icefields Pkwy

Scenic drive day

One of the most scenic drives on Earth: Bow Lake, Crowfoot Glacier, and Peyto Lake Viewpoint. Turn back at Saskatchewan River Crossing, or push on to the Columbia Icefield if you're up for a long day.

05Banff area

Choose your adventure

Lake Minnewanka cruise or the Tunnel Mountain/Hoodoos hike, free afternoon for Canmore/shopping/spa, last dinner in Banff.

06Banff → Toronto

Fly to Toronto

Drive back to Calgary, fly Calgary→Toronto, check in, easy evening at the Distillery District.

07Toronto

CN Tower & the Islands

CN Tower early to beat the lines, Harbourfront, ferry out to the Toronto Islands for the afternoon.

08Toronto

Museums & neighborhoods

Royal Ontario Museum, Casa Loma, then Kensington Market & Graffiti Alley.

09Niagara Falls

Day trip to the Falls

About 1.5 hours from Toronto. Hornblower boat cruise to the base of the Horseshoe Falls, dinner in Niagara-on-the-Lake.

10Toronto

Market & free day

St. Lawrence Market in the morning, free afternoon for shopping/neighborhoods (Queen West, Yorkville) or a ball game if the Jays are in town.

11Toronto

Flex day

Rest day, second Niagara wine-country outing, or anything you missed. Pack up.

12Depart

Fly home

Depart Toronto Pearson (YYZ).

06Banff → Québec

Fly to Québec City

Fly Calgary→Montréal, then transfer to Québec City (VIA Rail ~3h, or rental car ~2h45). Check into Old Québec.

07Québec City

Old Québec walking day

Château Frontenac & Dufferin Terrace, Petit Champlain, the funicular, the Citadelle.

08Québec City

Falls & Île d'Orléans

Montmorency Falls (taller than Niagara!) in the morning, farm stands and cidery tastings on Île d'Orléans in the afternoon — strawberries peak in late July.

09Québec → Montréal

Travel day

Train to Montréal (~3h), check in, evening wander through Old Montréal & the Old Port.

10Montréal

Basilica & the Port

Notre-Dame Basilica, the Old Port (Ferris wheel), the Jacques-Cartier Bridge area.

11Montréal

Mountain & markets

Mount Royal Park lookout, Jean-Talon Market, the Plateau Mont-Royal neighborhood.

12Depart

Fly home

Depart Montréal-Trudeau (YUL).

Good to know

Before you go

🎟️Book your Moraine Lake / Lake Louise shuttle now. Private vehicles are banned on Moraine Lake Road. Parks Canada releases reservations in waves (40% in advance, the remaining 60% on a rolling 2-day-ahead basis) — for a mid/late-July trip, check reservation.pc.gc.ca immediately and keep checking the 48-hour release if your date shows sold out.
💸Check the Canada Strong Pass. Parks Canada has indicated this pass returns for summer 2026 with free admission to national parks and historic sites — worth confirming before you buy a separate Banff park pass.
✈️Book flights early. Mid-to-late July is peak Canadian summer travel. Both routes fly out of Calgary (YYC) — fares typically rise the closer you get to departure.
🚗Rent a car for the Banff leg. Roam Transit covers the townsite, but the Icefields Parkway and most trailheads need your own wheels (or a guided tour).
🌤️Pack layers. Banff in July runs roughly 8–23°C and mountain weather turns fast — bring a rain shell even on a sunny forecast. Toronto/Québec/Montréal run warm and humid, upper 20s°C.
🗣️Route B language note. Québec City and Montréal are predominantly French-speaking. English is common in tourist areas, but a few French phrases go a long way.
⛰️Altitude. Banff sits at ~1,400m and Lake Louise higher still. Take the first day easy, drink more water than usual, especially before any big hike.
🛂ID & entry. A valid passport is the simplest way to enter Canada by air — confirm requirements for every traveler in your group well ahead of time.
